Key TakeawaysThe manufacturing process begins with converting a 3D design into G-code for precision machinery.CNC Nesting units perform cutting, grooving, and vertical drilling in a single, high-efficiency operation.Automatic edge banding machines provide crucial moisture protection and aesthetic finish to board edges.Final assembly typically uses "three-in-one" connectors (eccentric wheel, bolt, and nut) for maximum stability.Step 1: The Digital BlueprintEvery project on dexo.com starts as a digital file. This isn't just a picture; it's a technical map. CAD software creates the drawing, which CAM software then translates into G-code—the instructions that drive the cutting tools along a scheduled path.Step 2: Nesting and CuttingThe primary machine in modern cabinet making is the Nesting CNC Router. It uses a vacuum table to fix the board firmly, then completes cutting, grooving (for back panels), and drilling vertical holes all at once. This ensures that every piece fits together with mechanical precision.Step 3: Edge Banding and RefiningRaw board edges (MDF, plywood) must be sealed. An Automatic Edge Banding Machine applies a thin strip (PVC, wood veneer, etc.) using hot-melt adhesive. This process includes pre-milling to smooth the edge, gluing, pressing, and fine trimming to ensure the band is perfectly flush with the board's surface.Step 4: Final Assembly and HardwareCustom furniture in 2026 often relies on Three-in-One Connectors. This hardware consists of an eccentric wheel, a bolt, and a nut, providing a rigid connection that is more durable than standard screws. After cutting and banding, side holes are drilled to house these connectors, allowing the piece to be assembled (and disassembled if moving) with professional results.StageProcessBenefitNestingVertical Cutting/DrillingHigh speed, no human errorEdge BandingSealing raw edgesMoisture protection, longevitySide DrillingAssembly prepPerfect hardware alignmentAssembly3-in-1 HardwareStructural integrity
