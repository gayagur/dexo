### SketchUp: The Industry Standard for Quick Modeling

SketchUp has been the go-to tool for architects and interior designers since its acquisition by Trimble. Its "push-pull" modeling paradigm is intuitive enough to learn in an afternoon, and the 3D Warehouse provides millions of free models that you can drop directly into your scene.

**Strengths:**
- Massive community and model library
- Intuitive push-pull modeling metaphor
- Excellent for spatial planning and room layouts
- Extensions ecosystem adds specialized features (e.g., CutList for woodworkers)

**Limitations:**
- The free web version lacks advanced rendering and export options
- Not parametric: changing a dimension requires manual re-modeling
- Pro version ($129/year) needed for commercial use and DWG export

**Best for:** Interior designers creating room layouts, architects adding furniture to building models, and hobbyists who want quick visualization without technical complexity.

### Blender: The Powerhouse for Photorealistic Visuals

Blender is a full 3D creation suite that rivals commercial software costing thousands of dollars. Its Cycles rendering engine produces photorealistic images with accurate light physics, and its Eevee engine provides real-time viewport rendering for faster iteration.

**Strengths:**
- Completely free and open source with no feature restrictions
- Industry-leading rendering quality
- Sculpting, animation, and compositing in one package
- Massive community with thousands of free tutorials and add-ons

**Limitations:**
- Steep learning curve; expect 40-80 hours before becoming productive
- Not optimized for precision dimensioned modeling
- No built-in furniture-specific workflows or material libraries
- Requires a dedicated GPU (4GB+ VRAM minimum) for comfortable rendering

**Best for:** Furniture designers who need catalog-quality product renders, 3D artists creating visualization scenes, and manufacturers who want photorealistic marketing images. For understanding how AI is accelerating these workflows, see our article on [AI in furniture design](/blog/ai-furniture-design-2026).

### FreeCAD: Engineering-Grade Parametric Modeling

FreeCAD uses parametric modeling, meaning every shape is defined by a sequence of dimension-driven operations. Change one measurement and the entire model updates accordingly. This is essential for production furniture where a client might request a bookshelf that is 10cm wider without changing the overall proportions.

**Strengths:**
- True parametric modeling with full edit history
- Exports to STEP, IGES, and other engineering formats
- Active development community with regular updates
- Part Design and Assembly workbenches handle complex multi-part furniture

**Limitations:**
- Interface feels dated compared to commercial alternatives
- Occasional stability issues with complex models
- Steep initial setup: choosing the right workbench configuration is confusing for beginners

**Best for:** Furniture engineers and manufacturers who need precise, repeatable designs with production-grade file exports.

### Sweet Home 3D: Room Planning Made Simple

Sweet Home 3D focuses on one thing: helping you plan room layouts with furniture placement. You draw walls on a 2D floor plan, drop in furniture from the included catalog, and instantly see a 3D walkthrough of the result.

**Strengths:**
- Extremely easy to learn (15-minute onboarding)
- Includes a library of common furniture and fixtures
- Simultaneous 2D plan and 3D view
- Available on Windows, Mac, Linux, and as a web app

**Limitations:**
- Cannot create custom furniture models from scratch
- Limited rendering quality
- No manufacturing output (dimensions, cut lists)

**Best for:** Homeowners planning room layouts before ordering furniture. Works well as a first step before moving to DEXO for custom piece design.

## Key Features to Compare

### Dimensioned Modeling

Furniture requires precise measurements. Not all 3D tools handle dimensions equally:

- **Parametric tools** (FreeCAD, Fusion 360, Shapr3D): Dimensions drive the model. Change 600mm to 700mm and every connected feature updates.
- **Direct modeling tools** (SketchUp, Blender): You push and pull geometry to approximate dimensions, then type exact values. Changes do not propagate automatically.
- **Template-based tools** (DEXO, Sweet Home 3D): You select a furniture type and input dimensions directly. The software handles the 3D geometry.

### Material and Texture Libraries

Visualizing your furniture with realistic materials matters for making decisions:

- **DEXO:** Curated library of real production materials (wood veneers, laminates, edge banding) that match what manufacturers actually stock
- **SketchUp:** Materials from 3D Warehouse vary wildly in quality
- **Blender:** Thousands of free PBR materials available from sites like Poly Haven, but you must download and apply them manually

### Manufacturing Output

If your goal is not just visualization but actually building or ordering the furniture:

- **DEXO:** Generates cut lists, edge banding schedules, and CNC-compatible files automatically
- **Fusion 360:** Full CAM integration for generating toolpaths
- **SketchUp + CutList extension:** Basic cut list generation through a community plugin
- **Blender, Sweet Home 3D:** No manufacturing output; visualization only

The right tool depends entirely on your goal:
